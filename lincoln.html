Abraham Lincoln

Abraham Lincoln - Key Events

  • 1809 Born in Sinking Spring Farm, Kentucky, U.S.
  • 1842 Married to Mary Todd
  • 1846 Won election to the U.S. House of Representatives
  • 1860 Elected president of the United States
  • 1861 Lincoln was inaugurated as 16th U.S. president
  • 1861 Civil War Begins - "The bloodiest war in American history"
  • 1864 Reelected president
  • 1865 Thirteenth Amendment - Abolition of Slavery. "Neither slavery nor involuntary servitude, except as a punishment for crime whereof the party shall have been duly convicted, shall exist within the United States, or any place subject to their jurisdiction"
  • 1865 Second inauguration
  • 1865 End of the Civil War - Confederate General Robert E. Lee surrenders
  • 1865 On April 14, Actor and Confederate sympathizer John Wilkes Booth shoots President Lincoln at Ford's Theater in Washington, D.C., at around 10:15 P.M.
    Lincoln was removed to the Petersen boarding house across the street from the theater, where he died at 7:22 A.M. April 15
